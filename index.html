History

Irish dancing is a traditional form of dance that originated in Ireland. It is known for its rapid leg and foot movements, while the body and arms remain mostly stationary. The dance has roots in ancient Celtic traditions and has evolved over centuries, gaining worldwide popularity through shows like Riverdance.

Styles of Irish Dance

  • Stepdance: Characterized by precise, quick footwork and a rigid upper body. Dancers often wear elaborate costumes and wigs.
  • Ceili Dance: Group dances performed in social settings, often at celebrations and festivals.
  • Set Dance: Danced in square formations, similar to quadrilles, with couples performing intricate patterns.

Fun Facts

  • Irish dance competitions are called feiseanna (singular: feis).
  • Hard shoes in Irish dance are similar to tap shoes and create rhythmic sounds.
  • Riverdance, first performed in 1994, brought Irish dance to a global audience.
